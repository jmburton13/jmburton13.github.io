Welcome to the Vendomatic 8000!

Only four weeks into my coding bootcamp, I teamed up with a partner via Git VCS and we built a program that could populate its inventory using File I/O, display that inventory dynamically following purchases, track money fed and distribute change in the fewest amount of coins.

In addition, the program keeps an audit log that is appended with every purchase, with the record persisting even through multiple runs of the program. As an additional challenge, the program outputs a sales log each time it is run, which tracks and writes information more appropriate for a business audience, listing all products and their sales numbers, revenue from each, and the total revenue for the machine.

Why a Pig Latin Translator, Jake?

After wrapping up a lesson on CLI applications, I decided to play with user input
and string manipulation based on atypical rules. I have two ongoing endeavors here:

1. Implement more of the true, complex Pig Latin translation rules

2. Excercise asking proper UX questions about this made-up translator
such as "How might you move the syllables around for languages where a whole
word is only one character?" and "Do users expect proper translation to English
of improperly entered Pig Latin?" -- These may seem silly, but what's important
to me is the excercise in making a product for a user other than myself.
